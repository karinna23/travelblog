What is Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas?

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) located in Quezon City serves as the main branch of the country's central monetary authority. It is responsible for maintaining price stability, financial system integrity, and efficient payments and settlements in the Philippines. The BSP formulates and implements monetary policy, supervises banks and financial institutions, and ensures a safe and stable financial system. The Quezon City complex also hosts the Security Plant Complex, where the countryâ€™s currency and security documents such as banknotes and coins are produced. The institution plays a vital role in fostering economic growth and development by ensuring trust in the financial system and promoting financial inclusion.

During our visit to the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) in Quezon City, we had the opportunity to explore and understand the four major phases involved in the production of Philippine currency. A BSP representative guided us through the process, starting with the design phase, where artistic elements, national symbols, and security features are planned. The second is the pre-press and plate-making phase, where the approved designs are transferred onto printing plates. The third is the printing phase, where high-security machines produce the banknotes with features like watermarks, tactile marks, and threads. The final stage is quality control and issuance, ensuring that each note meets strict standards before distribution.
