What is Metropolitan Manila Development Authority?

The Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA), known in Filipino as Pangasiwaan sa Pagpapaunlad ng Kalakhang Maynila, is a government agency in the Philippines that oversees the regional governance of Metro Manila, which includes Manila, Quezon City, Caloocan, Pasay, Mandaluyong, Makati, Pasig, Marikina, Muntinlupa, Las PiÃ±as, ParaÃ±aque, Valenzuela, Malabon, Taguig, Navotas, San Juan, and the municipality of Pateros. It operates under the direct supervision of the Office of the President, carrying out planning, monitoring, and coordination tasks. The MMDA also holds regulatory and supervisory authority over the delivery of services across Metro Manila, while still respecting the autonomy of local government units on purely local matters. The agency is led by a chairman appointed by the President, who holds the rank, rights, and privileges of a cabinet member and serves at the President's discretion.
