What is Presidential's Car Museum?

Here is a paraphrased version of the paragraph with appropriate parts bolded using The Presidential Car Museum (Filipino: Museo ng Pampangulong Sasakyan) is located inside the Quezon Memorial Circle in Quezon City, Metro Manila. This museum features a collection of vehicles once used by former Philippine Presidents. Plans to establish a dedicated museum for these historical cars began as early as 2013, spearheaded by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP). A site for the museum was secured through a memorandum of agreement between the NHCP and the local government of Quezon City. The city allocated approximately 3,000 square meters for the museum's construction. The designated area, originally used for basketball and volleyball courts, was relocated within the park with expenses covered by the NHCP. The museum was officially opened on August 19, 2018.

During our visit to the Presidential Car Museum, we were accompanied by a knowledgeable tourist guide who walked us through the exhibits and provided detailed explanations about each historical vehicle on display. As we roamed around the museum, the guide shared fascinating stories about the former Philippine presidents who used the cars, their historical significance, and how each vehicle reflected the era it represented. The guided tour made the experience more meaningful and engaging, helping us appreciate the rich history preserved within the museum walls.
